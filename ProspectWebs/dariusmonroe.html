Analysis

Overview

Big, strong interior lineman who carries his weight well and doesn't look like much of a project from a physical standpoint. Monroe is a productive lead blocker with the size and skill to become an NFL starter. He's well-built, runs his feet and does a good job of driving into defenders. He also plays the game with enough toughness and aggression that he can be used as a goal line runner or short yardage specialist on occasion.

Strengths

  • Awareness in pass protection to work around rush moves and avoid big hits.
  • Has experience working inside or outside at point of attack.
  • Plays through defenders without letting them disrupt his footwork.
  • Uses length and power to drive tackles back off the point of attack.
  • Flashes solid understanding of how to use his feet to keep base firm and center of gravity low.

Weaknesses

  • Lacks explosiveness out of his stance when run blocking.
  • Tends to fall backwards instead of forward when finishing blocks.
