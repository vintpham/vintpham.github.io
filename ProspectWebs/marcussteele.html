Player Bio

Won Nagurski Trophy (nation's top defender), Bednarik Award (top defensive player) and Bronko Nagurski National Defensive Player of the Year. Set school single-season record with seven interceptions. Tied for second nationally in INTs/game (0.67).

Analysis

Overview

Steele was part of an athletic group that came through Louisiana Tech over the past few years and made college football fans take notice while playing big roles in the Bulldogs' turnaround under coach Sonny Cumbie. He has been on the rise since his freshman season but finally took off this year after becoming a full-time starter. Steele's ball production jumps out right away, but it is just average once the ball gets in the air. His lack of consistency makes it difficult for him to trust that he will make plays consistently even if everything else around him is working well. Strengths

  • Well built with long arms for his size.
  • Possesses the ability to play both inside or outside at cornerback.
  • Plays with good body control, allowing him to stay in phase on deep throws.
  • Willing to step up and challenge bigger targets who come across his face.
  • Good feel for maintaining leverage on receivers coming back toward the sideline.
  • Efficient and effective in taking proper angles and using hands to disrupt catches.
  • Quick feet allow him to reposition himself to redirect routes underneath.
  • Adequate competitor and willing tackler near the line of scrimmage.
  • Improved ability to read eyes and anticipate movements from quarterbacks.

Weaknesses

  • Struggles to transition quickly from man coverage into zone.
  • Lacks fluidity when turning and running with receivers downfield.
  • Feet get heavy when he tries to slow down and mirror the receiver's route breaks.
  • Needs better awareness of where the quarterback is located during catch windows.
  • Below-average burst to close gaps on vertical routes.
  • Gets too caught up trying to force turnovers rather than limiting damage.
  • Trails behind receiver's break points far too often.
  • Footwork becomes choppy when forced to change directions laterally.
