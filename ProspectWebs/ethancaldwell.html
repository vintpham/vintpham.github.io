Analysis

Overview

Three-down defender who has the size and play strength to handle the run game but can also rush from a two-, three- or four-point stance. He's not as twitchy as an edge rusher but can still win around the corner when he lands his punch on the outside arm of the blocker, creating leverage pressure for himself while working downhill toward the quarterback.His ability to bull-rush and create inside disruption is well below average and could be a major concern when it comes time to set up a pass rush plan at the next level. His hand usage is somewhat limited and doesn't have much counter move work off those hands.Calwell will need some coaching to become a more consistent impact player, but there are traits to suggest that he could develop into an effective role piece who can provide value against both the run and the pass.

Strengths

  • Long arms and big frame provide good length through contact.
  • Experienced starter capable of playing multiple techniques and rushing from various alignments.
  • Has the anchor power to hold back blockers trying to steer him away from target area.
  • Plays with active feet and shortens distance to disrupt early.
  • Uses upper body to control blocker once they engage.

Weaknesses

  • Needs to find better pad level as point of attack rusher.
  • Average foot quickness plays catch-up getting into lateral pursuit flow.
  • Slow acceleration out of turns can lead to missed attempts at chase plays.
  • Consistency and effort aren't always there over the course of a full game.
  • Pass sets lack consistency in placement and timing.
